Canton, China

In Canton, China, families come together during the Lunar New Year to celebrate with a feast known as the "Reunion Dinner." This important meal is held on New Year's Eve and is centered around dishes symbolizing prosperity, such as dumplings, fish (representing abundance), and noodles (for long life). It's a time to reflect on the past year and wish for good fortune in the future.

Sicily, Italy

In Sicily, Italy, family gatherings are an essential part of daily life, especially on Sundays when families sit down for large meals that often feature homemade pasta, fresh bread, and locally sourced produce. One cherished tradition is the "Festa di San Giuseppe," celebrated on March 19, where families come together to share a feast in honor of Saint Joseph, the patron saint of Sicily.

Alamut, Iran

In the Alamut region of Iran, family gatherings often revolve around "Shab-e Yalda," the celebration of the longest night of the year, where families share fruits, nuts, and pomegranates as they recite poetry and reflect on ancient Persian traditions. This night represents the triumph of light over darkness, and the food symbolizes health and prosperity for the coming year.
